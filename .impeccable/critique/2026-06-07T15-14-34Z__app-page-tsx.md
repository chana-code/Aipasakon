## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** This does NOT read as AI slop. It has a genuine point of view: a hand-drawn line-art avatar of the founder, floating doodles (star, flower, paper plane), a sticky-note widget, a click-to-play lo-fi player. That is real personality, not a template. It avoids every cliché in the project's anti-reference list except two it quietly reintroduces (see below).

**Deterministic scan:** UNAVAILABLE — the bundled detector failed to load (`detect.mjs`: "bundled detector not found"). Manual review substituted. Two self-inconsistencies surfaced against the project's own `PRODUCT.md` anti-references:
- Hero has two `blur-3xl rounded-full` **glow blobs** (Hero.tsx:49–50). PRODUCT.md explicitly bans "glowing effects" and "decorative blobs."
- Hero background is a **3-stop gradient** (paper → teal-50 → mark-bg). Borderline against the "Restrained" color strategy; warm-toned so not the banned purple, but louder than the system prescribes.

No `background-clip:text` gradient text, no side-stripe borders, no identical icon-grid, no glassmorphism cards, no hero-metric template. Those bans hold.

## Overall Impression

The hero is the strongest thing here — warm, human, confidently illustrated, and unmistakably *this* author. Mobile is clean (zero horizontal overflow, 56px touch targets, headline reflows to 32px). The real problems are not visual taste; they are (1) a typography system that has silently drifted from the design system it claims to follow, and (2) an unresolved strategic tension between "playful personal-brand landing" and the brand's stated #1 law, "the book test." Fix the drift, make a deliberate call on the book-vs-landing question, and this is a genuinely distinctive page.

## What's Working

1. **Authentic, non-generic hero.** The line-art avatar + doodle ecosystem + lo-fi widget give the page a personality no template produces. For a single-author trust play, this is the right instinct.
2. **Mobile discipline.** 375px: no overflow, headline drops to 32px/1.2, primary CTA is a 56px-tall pill. The absolutely-positioned avatar and doodles stay inside the viewport (avatar right edge 351 < 375).
3. **Voice-product fit.** "สวัสดีครับ ผมอ๋อง / ผมทำให้ AI เข้าใจง่าย เป็นภาษาคน" — copy and design agree on exactly the audience PRODUCT.md describes.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] Typography has drifted off the design system.** The landing components hardcode `font-['Noto_Serif_Thai',serif]` and `font-['DM_Sans',sans-serif]`, but `globals.css` (updated today) bundles only **Prompt + JetBrains Mono**. Result, verified live: the `<h1>` computes to `Prompt` while the greeting `<p>` computes to `"Noto Serif Thai", serif` — two different typefaces in the same hero. Those two fonts only render correctly because *this* machine happens to have them installed; a typical Thai visitor will get generic serif/sans fallbacks. Same pattern exists in `app/learn/page.tsx` and `app/archive/page.tsx`.
- **Why it matters:** The #1 brand promise is "feel like a well-edited book." Inconsistent, machine-dependent type is the fastest way to break that trust, and it breaks invisibly (looks fine to the author, wrong to visitors).
- **Fix:** Delete the `Noto_Serif_Thai` / `DM_Sans` font classes from all landing/learn/archive components and let them inherit Prompt (or set explicit Prompt utilities). One find-and-replace per file.
- **Suggested command:** `typeset`

**[P2] The homepage contradicts the brand's own #1 law.** PRODUCT.md: "The book test… If it looks like a landing page, it's wrong." This is, structurally, a personal-brand landing page (hero + specialty band + card grid + about). It may be the right exception (a book still has a cover and an author intro), but right now it is unresolved rather than decided.
- **Why it matters:** The calm-textbook reading experience and the playful animated front door set two different expectations for trust. If a visitor arrives expecting depth and meets a bouncy landing page, the handoff can feel like a bait-and-switch.
- **Fix:** Make an explicit decision and document it in PRODUCT.md (e.g., "homepage is the cover; interior is the book"). If kept playful, add one visible bridge element that previews the calm reading surface so the transition is honest.
- **Suggested command:** `shape`

**[P2] Self-inconsistency with the anti-reference list.** The two hero glow blobs and the 3-stop gradient are precisely the "glowing effects / decorative blobs / gradients" the project bans for everyone else.
- **Why it matters:** A design system loses authority the moment its flagship page exempts itself. Small, but it's the canary for drift.
- **Fix:** Replace the glow blobs with a single flat warm wash or remove them; reduce the hero gradient to a 2-stop or solid paper. Let the doodles carry the warmth.
- **Suggested command:** `quieter`

**[P3] Auto-rotating headline has no pause control.** SpecialtyBand cycles four phrases every 2.6s. Reduced-motion users get a static first phrase (good), but motion-tolerant users cannot pause or step through.
- **Why it matters:** WCAG 2.2.2 (Pause/Stop/Hide) applies to auto-updating content that runs longer than 5s. It also steals attention from the CTAs directly above.
- **Fix:** Pause on hover/focus, or swap the auto-rotation for a single confident line.
- **Suggested command:** `animate`

## Persona Red Flags

**Ying (first-timer, zero AI background — the core PRODUCT.md persona):** Lands on a warm, friendly hero — good, anxiety is lowered. But "Pro Usage" on the sticky note is untranslated English jargon on a page whose whole promise is "ภาษาคน (plain human language)"; for a true beginner it's a small but real "this isn't for me" signal. The four cards rescue her with plain-Thai descriptions.

**Nat (mobile commuter, reading on a phone on the BTS):** Strong experience — no horizontal scroll, large tap targets, headline reflows cleanly. Risk: the hero animations and lo-fi player assume a steady connection and steady hand; on a jittery connection the opacity:0 → whileInView reveal means body sections are briefly invisible until JS + IntersectionObserver fire. Works, but content visibility depends on client JS with `once:true` and no no-JS fallback.

**Som (skeptic, burned by AI hype):** The human, non-corporate hero earns initial trust. But the glow blobs + gradient are exactly the "every AI site" visual vocabulary she's tired of; they slightly undercut the "not selling dreams (ไม่ขายฝัน)" promise made lower on the same page.

## Minor Observations

- SpecialtyBand uses a fixed-height clip (`h-[44px]`/`h-[60px]`) for rotating phrases; a longer phrase would be cut off.
- Dev server emitted repeated "Fast Refresh: unrecoverable error → full reload" warnings during inspection. No runtime console *errors* on the page, but the instability is worth a look (often a hydration mismatch or the mix-blend-mode avatar).
- The bundled slop detector is broken (`detect.mjs`: "bundled detector not found") — the automated half of this critique couldn't run. Worth fixing so future audits are repeatable.
- Card hover lift uses `transform`/`translate` (fine) and shadow — consistent with the motion rules. Good.

## Questions to Consider

- Is this homepage the *cover of the book* or *a landing page*? The answer should be deliberate, and PRODUCT.md should record it.
- If the doodles and avatar already carry all the warmth, what are the glow blobs and the 3-stop gradient still doing?
- What would a visitor on a 3G phone with slow JS see in the first second, and is that the impression you want?
